A named pipe is a mechanism that enables interprocess communication for applications to communicate locally or remotely. The appication that creates the pipe is known as the pipe server, and the application that connects to the pipe is known as the pipe client. Similar to sockets, after the server creates the named pipe, pipe clients may connect to the server"

Named Pipes Access Rights
Access is defined by a security descriptor
The ACLs in the default security descriptor for a named pipe grant full control the the LocalSystem account, administrators, and the creator owner. They also grant read access to members of the Everyone group and the anonymous account.
